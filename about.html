My name is Junpei Zhong (traditional Chinese: 鍾俊培, simplified Chinese: 钟俊培). I was born and grew up in the third largest city in China, Guangzhou, locating in the southern part. In year 2002, I attended South China University of Technology, which is also in Guangzhou city. There I studied Control Science (major) and Computer Science (minor). Besides of taking required classes, I also took part in other research projects (such as RobotCup, embedded system design). I think my research interests started since that time.

In the year 2006, because I reached 10% of the overall ranking of all the exams, I had a chance to continue to post-graduate study without taking the postgraduate admission examination (which should be essential for most undergraduate students if they want to continue studying in graduate schools in China) . From the fall of 2006 to summer of 2007, I was in Harbin Institute of Technology Shenzhen Graudate School where I studied more advanced classes and conducted basic research experiments, but mostly in Mechatronics and Control parts.

However, in the year 2007, I got another offer from the Hong Kong Polytechnic University so I moved more south with much better facilities and supports. I did research in a biologically SLAM algorithm on multiple robots. The problem of solving a multi-robot SLAM is equal to solve a fairly high-dimensional problem. However, I tried to solve it "softly" with Ant Colony Optimization (Please see my research section). It was my first time to utilize a biological-AI method to solve an engineering problem: the art of a biology phenomenon elegantly solves a difficult problem in an easy way.

After I realised the power of a biological system and its inspiration to solve difficult problems, I was would really interested in the power of brain that is assumed to be even more powerful. Therefore I joined University of Hamburg with RobotDoC Collegium, which is a multi-national doctoral training network on developmental robotics. More specifically, our part in the WTM group of Hamburg belongs to the "Brain Node", which focus on computational neuroscience and its embodiment in a Cognitive Robotics system.

Research Interests

My current research encompasses the following interrelated research themes:

Visual system modeling, and its relation into the affordance learning in a mirror neuron system.

Bootstrap Learning for Visual Perception on Robots.

My previous research also includes SLAM, and its biological-inspired solutions.
